## 7. ROOT CAUSE DIAGNOSIS: Why Revenue Is Declining

### The Core Problem: Black Owned is Deflating

Black Owned was the $50K/mo brand. Now it's a $20K/mo brand (outside Q4). That single brand accounts for **most of the portfolio decline**. The business didn't uniformly shrink ‚Äî Black Owned collapsed while Card Plug quietly grew.

### Contributing Factors:

1. **Ad Spend Inefficiency (Black Owned):** $70K/yr in ads generating diminishing returns. ACOS worsened from ~17% to 26% in summer. Money is being burned on keywords that don't convert.

2. **Catalog Bloat:** 49 SKUs at Black Owned, 45 at Kinfolk. Most aren't selling. Dead SKUs dilute listing quality signals, waste storage fees, and spread ad spend too thin.

3. **Traffic Loss:** Sessions dropped 50%+ at Black Owned Feb‚ÜíJan. This suggests either:
   - Amazon algorithm deprioritization (too many low-BSR listings)
   - Competitor gains in the niche
   - Reduced ad visibility despite maintained spend

4. **No New Winners:** The last clear hit was Hood Charades (2022-era). No new product has broken through to replace declining performers. The portfolio is aging without refresh.

5. **Market Saturation:** The Black party games niche on Amazon is more crowded now. Original first-mover advantage has eroded.

6. **Seasonal Dependency:** 36% of annual revenue in Nov+Dec is dangerous. Any Q4 disruption (stockouts, algorithm changes, competition) is existential.

### The Honest Summary:
The business went from ~$110K/mo (Q4 2024 pace) to ~$60K/mo (mid-2025) because Black Owned's organic flywheel broke. Ad spend couldn't compensate. Card Plug is the only brand executing well. Kinfolk has too many SKUs and not enough winners.
