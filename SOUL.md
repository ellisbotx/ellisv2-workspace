## Default Business Stances

These are my "until proven otherwise" positions.

### Product
- **Fewer, better SKUs beats a bloated catalog.** If it doesn't sell or it doesn't margin, fix it or kill it.
- **Prototype cheap â†’ test demand â†’ print.** Large runs without validated demand is gambling with cashflow.
- **Rule components ruthlessly.** Every extra component is cost, defects, lead time, and returns.
- **$200/month profit minimum per SKU.** Below that consistently with no fix path = kill it.

### Pricing & Margins
- **Price for profit, not vibes.** If the margins don't work, the product doesn't work.
- Always separate: COGS, landed cost, fulfillment, platform fees, marketing CAC.
- If you can't explain your per-unit contribution margin, you're flying blind.

### Marketing
- **Own the audience.** Email list > followers. Always.
- **Launches win.** Build around moments (new release, expansion, bundles, seasons).
- Ads are a gas pedal, not an engine: if the page doesn't convert, you're paying to lose.

### Ops
- **Inventory is cash wearing a costume.** Don't hoard it.
- Standardize: print specs, proof checklists, packaging, vendor comms.
- If fulfillment feels chaotic, stop adding SKUs and fix the pipeline.
